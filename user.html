PERSONAS

OVERVIEW

As a part of our project in Design of Human Machine Interface, we invented seness. (More inforamtion about seness - Just click here) When developing user-friendly products, it‘s a great challenge to identify the needs, weaknesses and goals of one's target group. That's why it‘s useful to create so-called personas, which represent a fictitious personas part of a project.

TIME

Oct 2021 - Jan 2022

TOOLS

PROCESS

Bringing all the compontents of a persona together:

  • Biography

    A short descpription about her past and current life.

  • Goals

    Future goals she wants to accomplish.

  • Frustrations

    Event or circumstances that causes her to feel frustrated.

  • Personality

    Rating her on a scala of opposites: introvert vs. extrovert, non-technic vs. technic, inactive vs. active, sketic vs. optimistic

  • Hobbies

    An activity she enjoys doing in her spare time.

  • Quote

    A short sentence that could be said by the persona.

RESEARCH & TESTING

OVERVIEW

This project started with a concept called SPRYZE. The goal of it is to strengthen the relationship between influencers and their fans with challenges and micropayments. Our task was to rethink the idea and to develop a new design an interation concept for the platform. Following I describe the part, where we activly worked together with the user.

TIME

March 2022 - June 2022

TOOLS

PROCESS

Result of the User Research:

Our target group wasn't intereset in challenges at all. This is the reason why we created a concept, where fans get something back, in the form of qualtiy time with their favorite influencer.

Iterative process with possible users:

  • Step 1: Testing

    We designed observation sheet with several use cases to track our users thinkings and feelings. For our first User Testings, we used our paper prototype. We filtered between pain points, good points and wishes and suggestions.

  • Step 2: Implementing feedback

    We gathered the feedback of our User Testings on a Miro Board and checked whether some pain points had occured several times. All pain points were discussed and we tried to find fitting solutions.

  • Step 3: Prototyping

    We implemented our solutions into our low-fidelity prototype. We repeated the whole process (step one to three) twice till we finally came up with a presentionable result.
